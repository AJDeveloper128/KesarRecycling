About Us

Recycling Metals & EPS the Sustainable Way

At KesarBros Recycling, we’re redefining waste by giving new life to all types of metals and Expanded Polystyrene (EPS). Through innovative, eco-friendly recycling processes, we help businesses and communities reduce their environmental footprint while promoting a circular economy. From scrap metal to foam packaging, we ensure that valuable materials are recovered, responsibly processed, and reintroduced into the supply chain—keeping waste out of landfills and sustainability at the core of everything we do.

Join us in building a cleaner, greener future—one recycled material at a time.

Our Commitment to Sustainability

Accepted Materials

Steel and Iron

We recycle structural steel, iron castings, and heavy industrial scrap.

Copper & Brass

Includes pipes, wires, fittings, and all brass-based alloys.

Aluminum

From cans to sheets—aluminum of all grades is welcome.

Wire

Copper and aluminum electrical wiring, both insulated and bare.

Household Metals

Appliances, kitchen items, tools, and furniture metal parts.

Batteries

All types of household and industrial batteries, recycled safely.

E-waste

Computers, phones, cables, and other electronic waste.

EPS

We collect Expanded Polystyrene used in packaging and insulation.

About the Owner

I’m a young entrepreneur with a passion for sustainability and innovation, leading KesarBros Recycling, a dynamic recycling company dedicated to transforming waste into opportunity. Based in Canada and India, I founded this business to tackle environmental challenges, specializing in the collection of ferrous and non-ferrous materials.

My vision is to create a cleaner, greener future by partnering with municipalities and businesses to divert waste from landfills and promote a circular economy.

Driven by a commitment to excellence, I combine hands-on expertise with a forward-thinking approach, constantly exploring new technologies and partnerships to scale our impact. At KesarBros Recycling, we’re not just recycling—we’re redefining what’s possible for our planet, one tonne at a time.
